# Permissions and Allowed Uses

**Repository owner:** John Miller
**Contact:** john.miller@codestaffing.com

**Summary:** This file lists pre‑approved, limited permissions that do not require case‑by‑case negotiation, the process for requesting additional permissions, and templates for maintainers to record granted exceptions.

---

## Preapproved Uses (no negotiation required)
- **Noncommercial personal backups and archival copies**; must not be redistributed.
- **Linking to repository** from blogs, documentation, or social media; must include a link back to the repository.
- **Quoting up to 200 words** verbatim for reviews, commentary, or academic citation with attribution to the repository and a link.
- **Issue excerpts** posted publicly for debugging or support that include only the minimal necessary code snippets and a link to the issue.

---

## Uses That Require Written Permission
- **Any redistribution** of repository content in whole or in substantial part.
- **Use in commercial products, services, demonstrations, or training** where the repository materials are included, embedded, or sold.
- **Creating or publishing derivative works, adaptations, translations, or compilations.**
- **Bulk copying, mirroring, or inclusion in other repositories, package registries, or data dumps.**

---

## Forbidden Actions
- **Claiming ownership** of the repository or relicensing it under other terms without explicit written permission.
- **Removing or altering copyright or license statements** in repository files.
- **Automated scraping for commercial resale** of repository contents.

---

1. **Send email** to john.miller@codestaffing.com with subject: **Permission Request — [Repository Name] — [Short Purpose]**.
2. **Include** the following in the message body using the Request Template below.
3. **Expect a response** within a reasonable business timeframe from the repository owner.
4. **Do not proceed** with the intended use until written permission is received.

## Recording Exceptions (maintainers must append to this file)
- **Entry format (one line):** YYYY‑MM‑DD; Requestor name; Organization; Files; Scope summary; Granted/Denied; Expiration date; Permission ID.
- **Example entry:** 2025‑10‑16; ACME Labs; ACME Inc.; /docs/*.md; Redistribution for internal training only; Granted; 2026‑10‑16; PERM‑2025‑001.

---

## Attribution and Required Notices
- **When permission granted,** the requestor must include the exact attribution text provided in the grant and must not remove existing copyright notices.
- **If published online,** include a link to the repository and a copy of the grant message or permission ID.

## Fees and Additional Terms
- **Fees may be charged** at the discretion of the copyright holder for commercial uses.
- **Negotiated terms** may include limited sublicensing, license fees, or attribution requirements.
- **All granted permissions** are revocable for breach of terms; revocation will be communicated in writing.
